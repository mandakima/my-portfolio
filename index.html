Projects // Client & Personal Works

Here are some examples of recent projects I've worked on! If you're interested in seeing more work, send me a message at kim.amanda@outlook.com

Energized Electric LLC

Design & Development - Client

Energized Electric is a young and local electrician company working with some of Portland's top remodelers and restorers.


I was in charge of Phase 2 where I implemented the design created by the Senior Web Developer on Phase 1. Afterwards, phase 3 was passed to the Junior Developer for continuous implementation based on the client's requests.

Grove at 162nd

Design & Development - Client

The Grove at 162nd is a community of 26 detached, single-family homes that aims to help fulfill the housing goals of their plan by providing attainable housing options for working families and first-time home buyers.


I was in charge of Phase 1 and 2 by designing and implementing Zenith Pro's needs for their new, modern rental property. Afterwards, Phase 3 was passed to the Junior Developer for continuous implementation based on the client's requests.

Beela (Phase 4)

Development - Client

Our group of 5 developers collaborated with 5 designers and researchers to create a responsive website using SCSS, React.JS, and Typescript for Beela, a Swedish nonprofit that guides women and nonbinary people into technology.


The website with our implemented design went live on January 2023!

Develop for Good

Design - Client

Develop for Good is a nonprofit design agency that builds products for other nonprofits. Student volunteers gain valuable technical experience and nonprofits get quality digital services. I worked with two designers on the visual rebranding for Develop for Good in a lean UX environment.

E-Commerce Landing Page

Development - Personal

To gain a better understanding of React, I developed a landing page for a mock e-commerce website. Additionally, I learned styled-components, a more flexible way to style UI components by writing actual CSS in JavaScript.

Linkedin Post Generator

Development - Personal

For those who don't want to write up LinkedIn announcements for their new jobs, I created a simple post generator. This project was built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
